How should we frame the price to the Towkay?

RM12,000 setup + RM3,000/month is your current pricing. The question isn't the number — it's how the Towkay processes it. Which framing matches how your best deals closed?

A

"You'll save more than you spend"

Lead with ROI: "Most clients save RM8,000-15,000/month within 90 days. Your investment is RM3,000/month." Let the math do the selling. Show a before/after comparison for each industry.

Works when: the Towkay already feels the pain of wasted money (overstaffing, missed sales, manual errors)

B

"Cheaper than one staff member"

Frame it as headcount: "RM3,000/month = less than one junior admin's salary. But Boss OS works 24/7, never takes MC, never makes calculation errors, and manages 6 departments at once."

Works when: the Towkay thinks in staff cost. Every Malaysian boss knows hiring is expensive.

C

"Start small, grow when ready"

Offer a smaller entry point (e.g., RM5,000 setup + RM1,500/month for 2 departments). Towkay tries it on sales + accounting first. When they see results, upgrade to full 6-department Boss OS. Lower barrier, higher conversion.

Works when: the Towkay is interested but RM12K upfront feels risky for something they haven't seen

D

"Don't show price — make them WhatsApp you"

Show everything EXCEPT the price on the landing page. End with "Every business is different. WhatsApp us for a custom quote." This forces a conversation where you can sell personally and qualify the lead.

Works when: you close better in conversation than on a webpage. But you lose the Towkays who want to know price before talking to anyone.
